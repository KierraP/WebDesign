Gyeongbokgung Palace

Gyeongbokgung Palace is a historical Korean palace dating back to the 1300s. It is one of the palaces where Korean Kings and their families lived. It was completely destroyed by the Japanese and has been completely rebuilt and refurbished.

Visit Gyeongbokgung

My Experience

The palaces are very impressive in person, and the grounds are a lot more extensive than you would think. The funnest thing about the palace is that visitors are encouraged (and get free admission) if they rent Hanbok (traditional Korean clothes) from any of the nearby rental stores. Gyeongbokgung is one of two palaces in this area of Seoul, the other being Changdeokgung. Personally, I think Gyeongbokgung is prettier, so that's the one I would recommend if you only have time for one.

Travel

You can get to the National Museum of Korea via Seoul's metro. You can take Line 3 and get off at Gyeongbokgung Station. You can pay for individual trips, or you can load money onto a Wowpass or T-Money card to use the metro.

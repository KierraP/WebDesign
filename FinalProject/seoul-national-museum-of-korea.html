    • National Museum of Korea

    The National Museum of Korea is a Korean art and history museum located in the Yongsan District of Seoul.

    National Museum of Korea

    My Experience

    The exterior of the museum is beautifully landscaped, and honestly it's worth it just for that. Inside the museum is a deep dive into Korean history, complete with artifacts from each of its historical time periods, all the way up to modern day. Much like the rest of Seoul, all the signs and placards have Korean, English, Chinese, and Japanese language. I think it's really cool that everything is so accessible, to locals and tourists alike. Entry to the museum is free, but there are 1-2 temporary exhibitions that you have to pay for. You can also hit the Hangul (Korean alphabet) Museum next door, though it was closed for refurbishment during my trip in 2025.

    Travel

    You can get to the National Museum of Korea via Seoul's metro. You can take either Line 4 from Seoul Station or the Gyeongui-Jugang line from Yongsan Station. Get off at the Ichon stop, which opens up directly to the museum. You can pay for individual trips, or you can load money onto a Wowpass or T-Money card to use the metro.
